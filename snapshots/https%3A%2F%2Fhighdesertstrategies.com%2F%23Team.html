What We Do

High Desert Strategies (HDS) is a nonprofit working together with the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) to ethically and safely manage wild horses in our local backcountry of Eastern Oregon.

Herd Management & Health

Rangeland Management and Wild Horse Health are our primary goals. Our focus is managing population of wild horse herds through means of maintaining appropriate birth rates using the contraceptive GonaCon.

Maintaining Ecological Balance

Healthy rangelands are not only important to wild horse survival but also a wide variety of wildlife and many other resource users. Wild horses need to be managed in a manner that achieves a natural ecological balance.

Preserving Cultural Heritage

Wild horses are a part of the cultural heritage and history of the West, however they are often misunderstood and mismanaged. By helping fund critical range, fertility control you're helping us support wild horses' legacies.

Education & Awareness

At High Desert Strategies, our hope is to educate the public and spread awareness on wild horse herds in Eastern Oregon and the efforts to manage Appropriate Management Levels (AML) on the rangelands.

Wild Horse Management for Healthy Rangelands

High Desert Strategies (HDS) works alongside the BLM to strategically manage wild horses in a manner that promotes healthy rangelands and healthy herds. Our primary objective is to manage wild horse population growth by administering immuno-contraceptive vaccinations to wild horse herds in order to slow population growth in a manner that maintains healthy growth rates, genetic diversity, and is safe, cost effective, and low stress on the horses.

Working for a Better Future for Wild Horses & Rangelands

We are a great group of people working for the environmental health and humane treatment of wild horses. We believe that all horses deserve a safe and healthy life, and so we work hard to protect them and their land. At High Desert Strategies (HDS) we aim to protect, preserve and nurture wild horses in their native habitats.
