About Judith Pruzzo

Judith J. Pruzzo has over 30 years of experience and training in classical homeopathy. She is a licensed, registered pharmacist with five years of experience in hospital pharmacy, and is certified in Classical Homeopathy (C.C.H.) by the Council for Homeopathic Certification.  She studied extensively with the National Center for Homeopathy, George Vithoukas, M.H., world-renowned homeopath, and Paul Herscu, N.D. Other seminars have been with Massimo Mangialavori, M.D., Ananda Zaren, R.N., Prahash Vakil, M.D., Jan Scholten, Dr. Rajan Sankaran, Dr. Jayesh Shah, The International Foundation for Homeopathy, Maui Academy of Homeopathy, American Institute of Homeopathy and the Texas Society of Homeopathy as continuing homeopathic education.

Judith is a founding member, a frequent speaker, as well as a current board member of the Texas Society of Homeopathy and chairman and organizer of six annual Texas Society of Homeopathy conferences that were held in Dallas.

Three Principles

There are three main principles of prescribing in homeopathy:

1. Similia similibus currentur:
‍a Latin phrase meaning similars should cure similars. The homeopathic prescriber matches the symptom picture of the person, to that which is known to belong to various remedies (information) that has been obtained by drug provings).

2. Single remedy (in classical Homeopathy):
Only one remedy is given at one time until which time the symptoms change, denoting the need to change the prescription.

3. Minimum dose:
the smallest possible dose is given, with repetition of doses only when needed. If the remedy is acting and patient is feeling better and progressing toward health, wait to repeat the dose until improvement slows down or stops. Homeopathy may be your answer!
